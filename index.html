About

Currently, I am a postdoctoral research associate at the University of Sheffield (2021-present). My PhD in Biophysics and Cardiac Biology at the University of Leeds (2016-2021) was funded by the Medical Research Council (MRC), through the Discovery Medicine North Doctoral Training Partnership (DiMeN DTP). I previously completed an MPhil in Adaptive Organismal Biology at the University of Manchester (2015-2016).

My research harnesses biophysical imaging techniques (including fluorescence super-resolution techniques and electron microscopy) to quantify nanoscale biomolecular structures inside cells and multicellular organisms. By better understanding cellular structures we are able to decipher the structure-function relationships which are fundamental to the physical principles underlying biological systems.

Outside of the lab, I love hiking in the Peak district, running and going to gigs to enjoy live music.

Experiences

MRC innovation placement - Badrilla

12 months during PhD: 2019-2020

Research project - trialling novel imaging probes for the cardiac ryanodine receptor, using peptide ligands imperacalcin and DPc10. Pre-print published, access here.
eCommerce project - evaluating company website, involved with project management, business planning, intellectual property, marketing.

Research trip - University of Auckland

1 month during PhD: 2018

Studied remodelling in idiopathic dilated cardiomyopathy (IDCM) biopsies from human patients, by the application of 4x ExM. Many thanks to Dr David Crossman for welcoming me into his lab, and to the MRC DiMeN for the flexible fund grant.

Research assistant - Operation Wallacea, Cuba

1 month: 2015

Assisted with reef surveys to improve ecological understanding of the biodiversity of corals and Caribbean reef fish, research tackling invasive Lionfish species, and data collection regarding manatee distribution in relation to seagrass.

Summer studentship with the Society for Endocrinology - University of Liverpool

5 months: 2014

Project validating a non-invasive experimental refinement to measure levels of water-borne cortisol in fishtanks, as a less stressful alternative to improve fish welfare. Many thanks to Dr Lynne Sneddon and the SfE.
