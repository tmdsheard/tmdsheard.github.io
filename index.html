About me

I am a postdoctoral research associate at the University of Sheffield, on a UKRI-funded project developing new super-resolution microscopy techniques, including the hydrogel-based expansion microscopy (ExM), and testing them in a variety of life sciences projects to understand nanoscale biological structures.

My PhD at the University of Leeds, funded by the Medical Research Council (MRC), involved examining the structural basis of molecular-scale signalling and mechanical function of cardiac myocytes, using ExM. I gained the qualification of MPhil in Adaptive Organismal Biology at the University of Manchester, and my Bachelor's degree in Biological Sciences at the University of Leeds.

Outside of academic pursuits, I'm a keen runner, hockey player, and gig-goer.
